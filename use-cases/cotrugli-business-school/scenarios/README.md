# Generic scenarios

If you don't yet have your own business problem, use one of these four fictional scenarios to fill your `AGENTS.md` (via the [Fill-my-config prompt](../../../templates/prompts/fill-config.md) or by hand). The data is invented — adapt freely.

---

## Scenario 1 — Sales Report Optimisation
**Adriatica Wholesale d.o.o. — Sales Manager**

```
Problem:       Weekly sales report takes 4 hours every Friday. Data is pulled
               manually from three systems and reformatted in Excel, delaying the
               Friday review meeting.
Current state: 4 hours/week, report ready at 5 PM earliest
Target:        Under 60 minutes/week, report ready by noon
Reviewer:      Marija Novak, Sales Director

Allowed:   Research AI tools for data pulling · Redesign the template · Train one team member
Forbidden: Change report content without director approval · Share raw data externally
Evidence:  Time log (3 weeks before, 4 weeks after) · Workflow description with screenshots ·
           Written confirmation from reviewer that quality is maintained
```

## Scenario 2 — Customer Onboarding Improvement
**ProServ Consulting d.o.o. — Customer Success Manager**

```
Problem:       New client onboarding takes 18–22 business days. Clients report
               confusion; 3 delayed their second invoice last quarter due to friction.
Current state: 18–22 days average, 4+ status queries per onboarding
Target:        Under 8 days average, zero unsolicited status queries
Reviewer:      Luka Petrovic, Head of Sales

Allowed:   Map the current process · Design a new onboarding sequence · Test with 2 new clients
Forbidden: Change contract terms or payment conditions · Commit to delivery dates without approval
Evidence:  Current process map with timings · New process document ·
           Time logs from 2 test onboardings · Written feedback from ≥1 client
```

## Scenario 3 — Meeting Load Reduction
**TechGroup Zagreb d.o.o. — Head of Product**

```
Problem:       The product team spends 16 hours/person/week in meetings — mostly
               recurring status updates that could be async. Deep-work time is under
               2 hours/day per person.
Current state: 16 hours/person/week in meetings, <2h deep-work blocks/day
Target:        Under 8 hours/person/week, ≥3-hour deep-work blocks daily
Reviewer:      Ivan Bauer, CEO

Allowed:   Audit all recurring meetings · Design an async status process · Cancel reducible meetings
Forbidden: Cancel cross-functional meetings without aligning department heads ·
           Reduce 1:1s with direct reports
Evidence:  Calendar audit (meeting hours/person) · List of cancelled/converted meetings ·
           Calendar data after 4 weeks · Brief team feedback
```

## Scenario 4 — Invoice Processing Acceleration
**Kontura Manufacturing d.o.o. — Operations Manager**

```
Problem:       Supplier invoices take 4–6 business days from receipt to approval,
               causing 3–5 late-payment penalties per month. Manual data entry and
               Excel tracking.
Current state: 4–6 days processing, 3–5 late payments/month, manual Excel
Target:        Under 24 hours processing, zero late payments for 4 consecutive weeks
Reviewer:      Petra Simic, CFO

Allowed:   Map and time the current process · Test an AI-assisted invoice-reading tool ·
           Redesign the approval workflow
Forbidden: Change supplier payment terms without CFO approval · Access banking systems directly
Evidence:  Current process map with timings · Late-payment log (before) ·
           New process description and test results · Invoice log for 4 weeks after · CFO confirmation
```
